<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The final statement</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">‘I never held anti-Soviet views or convictions and never had the aim of weakening Soviet power.’</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I have been imprisoned for a year now and have had a great deal of time to think. I never held anti-Soviet views or convictions and never had the aim of weakening Soviet power.<br>I see my guilt in the fact that I did not think seriously enough about certain things; had I thought about them seriously enough at the time, I might not have found myself here, in the dock.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">In his closing speech, the prosecutor asked that a punishment be imposed on me involving deprivation of liberty for a term of one year. Therefore, I cannot ask the court for leniency. But regardless of what the sentence may be, I want to say that this, my first final word as a defendant, will also be my last.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>12 January 1968</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Moscow City Court, Moscow, USSR.</p>
