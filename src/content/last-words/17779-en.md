<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The final statement</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong><strong><strong><strong>‘This is my country, this is my home, I am responsible for it. My conscience is clear, I am not ashamed or afraid</strong></strong>‘.</strong></strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Throughout my life I managed to be a volunteer in different places. Somewhere I volunteered regularly, somewhere occasionally, somewhere I was officially registered, somewhere not. Somewhere I approached the matter very seriously, somewhere I was far from that…</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I have always had a goal to live a normal, calm, small and peaceful life. Since the beginning of the war, I have had two desires. The first one is to have a peaceful sky above our heads. I am not alone who has the same desire. I am supported by millions of citizens of our country. </p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The second desire is to, no matter what, stay in Russia till the end and help those who I can help. This is my country, this is my home, I am responsible for it. My conscience is clear, I am not ashamed or afraid. If I am found guilty by the court, one of my desires, I mean staying in Russia, no matter what happens later, will come true. Well, so be it.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Besides, as one good song goes: ‘Someone has to be on this side of the border’. </p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Thanks, I am ready.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Moscow City Court, Moscow, Russia</p>
